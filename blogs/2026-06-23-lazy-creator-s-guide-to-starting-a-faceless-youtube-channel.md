You do not need a camera setup, a perfect voice, or three weeks of editing tutorials to start testing YouTube. The easiest way to start a faceless YouTube channel is to pick one niche, write a simple script, turn that script into a video, add voiceover and captions, and then publish consistently enough to see what works. Async can speed up the testing stage by helping you move from idea or script to edited video without jumping between five different tools.
